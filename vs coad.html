The Two Lives


Now how could 1,with gold to spare.who

know the harlot's aems,and wine,Sit in

this green field all alone.

If nature was not mine?


That Pleasure life wakes stale at morn.

From hevy sleep that no rest brings: This

life of quiet joy wakes fresh.

And claps its wings at morn,and sings.


So here sit 1,alone till noon.

In one long dream fo quiet bliss:

I hear the lark and share his joy.

With no more winedrops than were his.


Such,Nature,is thy charm and power,

Since I have made the Muse my wife.

To keep me from the harlot's arms,

And save me form drunkard's life.


[William Henry Davies]
